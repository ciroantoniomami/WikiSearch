[edit] A word of advise

Always remember every time you hit the save button you leave a record that people can judge your work by. The current New changes (12/10/06 early morning) shows multiple edits of your talk page. Over 10 edits in 2 different batches trying to get things the way you wanted them. Each time you save, people see what you did, and with multiple saves, what you did wrong. Yes, it is a learning process. That being said, the preview button in most cases will prevent all your trials from being saved for people to hold against you. Everyone makes mistakes (anyone only has to look at the number of times I've commented Typo in one of my edits... oy..) but you should really try to limit that exposure as much as you can by using the preview button more. Be certain when you hit save you are happy with what you are saving and not just checking to see if the most current entry is better than what you had.

As to Blockinbox's thoughts on working on cats.. I would suggest against it for now. You really need more practice on general layout (as evident by your recent edits starting out with the wiki tag). Look to Category:Articles_that_need_to_be_wikified. Focus on the fundamentals of layout and links first. Understand cleaning up those articles as a basic level and progress into in:wiki linking, Images, external links, tables and the basics of Categories before trying to deal with something like the category tree itself. Work on Wikifying articles, upgrade to cleanup's (great for understanding layout structure) and articles such as the {{you}} Category:Articles in second person (nice for creative thinking on context). Learn the basics for strong editing before moving on to the next step. A good admin should be a strong editor first. Focus on mastering the basics of editing along with vandal patrols and understanding what this wiki is and how it needs to be. These are the steps to becoming someone people want as an admin. In the end, a great editor is better than an admin. A good editor doesn't have to be an admin, but an admin should always be a good editor first and foremost. Be the best editor you can be before focusing on being an admin.

Just some advice for you to consider. -- Creol 08:18, 10 December 2006 (UTC)
